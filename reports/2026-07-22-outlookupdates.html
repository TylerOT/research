HOW TO READ THIS REPORT

Everything here comes from one mechanical engine that watches 162 markets and marks turns: moments where price makes a new extreme but the internals stop confirming it. A turn at a low is a buy signal (accumulation); a turn at a high is a sell signal (distribution).

Each signal is described by five numbers. The anchor is the date and price where it printed - it doubles as the line that proves it wrong. The objective is where comparable past signals in that same market typically ended up (a historical median, not a prophecy). The clock is when they typically got there. Confluence counts how many of the engine's 57 independent indicators agreed at the turn - 5 is thin, 20 is solid, 30+ is rare. Degree says which timeframes agree: D is daily only, D+W adds the weekly, D+W+M means daily, weekly and monthly all point the same way - the strongest configuration this report assigns.

In the tables, hot means the market approached the turn faster than its own history (such signals have resolved harder), FLIPPED means the bias reversed since July 13, re-anchored means the same bias printed a fresh signal, and held means the July 13 reading still stands untouched. Objectives in the main board are measured from the daily signal; Section IX re-measures the strongest names from the weekly signal, one degree up.

VIII. The Anomalies - What Does Not Fit, and What Each One Means

A board this one-sided earns its credibility from the exceptions. Everything below is a reading that contradicts the main picture, taken seriously rather than smoothed over.

1. The sterling defection is the most important anomaly on the board. The July 13 thesis was dollar-down, and 77 percent of pairs still lean that way - but the pound flipped short across its entire complex on July 15, falling not just against the dollar but against the yen, the krona and the Singapore dollar at once. That is not a dollar move; it is a sterling move. And it changes what the dollar-down reading means: the currencies rising are no longer the risk-and-resource bloc broadly, they are increasingly the safe havens - franc, yen, gold. When a G4 risk-sensitive currency defects from an anti-dollar trade while the funding currencies rally, the trade is quietly changing character from reflation to risk-off. The discriminator is the euro: as long as EUR/USD holds its long toward 1.19-1.21, the dollar-down thesis survives in its original form. A euro flip following sterling would hand the entire currency board to the carry-unwind reading.

2. Full-degree longs living inside full-degree shorts. Palantir, Uber, Snap, Adobe and the energy fund XLE all carry buy signals confirmed on daily, weekly and monthly - the same maximum degree as the S&P and Nasdaq sell signals they coexist with. Both cannot be wrong. The reconciliation is that the index short is a concentration story: it is the banks, the defensives and now the first fortress name being distributed, while capital visibly accumulates elsewhere. This is a dispersion regime, not an everything-down regime - which is exactly what the rotation wave of July 15-17 showed in real time.

3. Microsoft is the board's largest unresolved contradiction. The last hot mega-cap accumulation, objective 504 on the daily and the low 700s at weekly degree, standing inside index sell signals at full degree. One of the two readings must give. It is scheduled to: Microsoft reports July 29, the same session as the Federal Reserve decision.

4. The energy split. Crude, Brent and gasoline carry buy signals with monthly support while heating oil flipped short at full degree on July 19. Distillate is the industrial-demand end of the barrel; when it inverts against a supply-driven crude rally, the market is pricing higher oil and weaker industrial activity at the same time. That is not a contradiction to this board - it is the stagflation configuration stated in energy terms.

5. Copper against the metals. Gold, silver, platinum and palladium are accumulating while copper carries a full-degree short toward 4.44-5.45. Same resolution: copper is the industrial metal and trades with the equity complex; the precious-metal rally is monetary, not industrial. The pair - long gold, short copper - is the cleanest single expression of the entire regime on the board.

6. Caterpillar flipped long while GE, GM and Boeing stayed short. An industrial cyclical printing accumulation inside an industrial distribution cluster is a genuine residual - it does not fit, and it is logged as such rather than explained away. One name against three; the sector call stands until a second industrial joins it.

7. The overdue clocks. The S&P, Alphabet and Tesla signals have all outlived their median duration without reaching their objectives. Overdue has two historical readings: a signal failing, or a signal compressing. The rule this report applies: overdue while price still respects the invalidation line is compression, not failure - and compressed resolutions have historically been the violent ones. Tesla answers first: it reports earnings July 22 with its clock already expired.

8. East Asia refuses the bear. Hong Kong and the China A50 remain the only major index longs, the A50 re-issuing its signal from a lower price after its July 13 stop was broken - an admitted miss that the market immediately re-argued. As of this week the anomaly acquires a mechanical catalyst, covered in the note below.

EXTERNAL CATALYST NOTE - CHINA ENDS RETAIL PAPER GOLD ON JULY 24

China's largest banks - ICBC, Postal Savings Bank, Ping An and China Guangfa, with China Construction Bank aligned earlier - terminate retail leveraged precious-metals trading linked to the Shanghai Gold Exchange after settlement on July 24, 2026. Existing clients must close, liquidate or take physical delivery by that date; physical gold, accumulation plans, ETFs and the exchange's institutional operations are unaffected. The wind-down was announced June 24-25, during the 30 percent decline from the January peak near 5,600, after margin requirements had been raised as high as 140 percent.

Three observations, without embellishment. First, the announcement dates sit five days before this board's gold buy signal of June 30 at 3,942.61 - the forced unwind of leveraged Chinese retail positions is a plausible mechanical author of the very capitulation the engine marked. Second, the deadline lands precisely inside this report's July 24-27 tell window and the gold gate week - a known, scheduled flow event coinciding with the board's own clock. Third, from July 25 onward a structural source of leveraged selling pressure in gold is simply gone, while the permitted channels - physical and ETFs - are the ones that tighten the metal. The report does not trade news; it notes when the news schedules itself onto the report's dates.

Weekly signals are rare, so samples are small; instruments with fewer than five completed weekly resolutions are listed separately as thin-sample references and should be read as direction, not destination.
